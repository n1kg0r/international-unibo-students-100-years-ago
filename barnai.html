Ibolya (Violetta) Barnai
(1915 —1993)
Studied Medicine and Surgery at the University of Bologna
Hungary
BIOGRAPHY

Ibolya (Violetta) Barnai was born on January 12th of January, 1915 in Budapest (Hungary) [1]. Her parents were Maurizio Barnai, a men's clothing merchant, and Olga Weisz, both Jewish. She also had an older brother whose name was Andrew Peter. Ibolya Barnai studied at the girls' secondary school in Budapest, then she spent a year studying at the girls' commercial course of the Israeli community. After that, Ibolya studied at the girls' high school "Zrinyi Ilona" from 1930 to 1933,“ merited in her moral conduct the note "exemplary"”.[2] Just before the WWII, Ibolya changed her name to Violetta .
Read more

At the age of 18, Violetta completed the school "Zrinyi Ilona". Because of the Numerus Clausus Act, which was introduced in 1920 in Hungary, she could not study in the Hungarian universities. The law formally placed limits on the number of minority students at university. Even though the text did not explicitly use the term “Jew”, it was clear at the time that the Jewish were nearly the only group overrepresented in higher education. Thus, Violetta Barnai was forced to study abroad[3], and she attended the Faculty of Medicine and Surgery in the University of Bologna, Italy. She studied there for 6 years. On 14th July 1938, the “Manifesto of Race” was published in “Il Giornale d’Italia”, after which Jewish people started being slowly excluded from public places and institutions. Because of that, Violetta was initially excluded from enrolling to her final year in Bologna University[4]. However, she sent two letters to Ministry of National Education and could prove that she had a right to continue studying, referring to the law issued in November 1938: “as a transitional measure, students of the Jewish race, already enrolled in institutions of higher education in past academic years, are admitted to continue their university studies”[5].

Violetta Barnai successfully finished her 6th year and on 20 November 1939 she passed the examination completing her degree in Medicine and Surgery, with the final mark corresponding to Eighty-Eight out of One Hundred and Ten (88/110)[6]. Ibolya Barnai got the degree of Doctor of Medicine and Surgery.

During World War II, Violetta was sent to internment camp Ferramonti in Calabria,[7] in southern Italy. Violetta survived and moved with her mother to Perugia in 1949. Her brother Andrew went to the USA and committed suicide there in 1949[8]. Violetta worked as a gynecologist and an endocrinologist in Perugia, and she died in 1993[9].
