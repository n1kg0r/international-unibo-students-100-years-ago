BIOGRAPHY

Szlama Josef Fromer was born on September 8th, 1914 in Częstochowa (Poland)[1]. His parents, named Abramo and Sura Klajman, were Jewish. He had two brothers named Emil and Lajzer (“Leon”) and a sister Tusia.

Read more
Szlama Josef Fromer studied at the gymnasium in Czestochowa from 1926 to 1933. The State Examining Board declared him mature for higher studies, awarding him this certificate[2]. Then Josef Fromer came to the University of Bologna in 1933 to study at the Faculty of Medicine and Surgery. He also spent a year in the Univetrsity of Genova and then came back to Bologna. He passed the medicine and surgery graduation exam with 100 points on July 3rd, 1939[3].

Szlama Josef Fromer could not get his Diploma because he was imprisoned in Buchenwald concentration camp in Germany as a political prisoner from the beginning of WWII. His family still lived in Częstochowa, when the war started. In 1942 his parents, Abramo and Sura Klajman, were sent to Treblinka extermination camp [4] and never came back. His sister Tusia could escape to Warsaw and was hiding, changing apartments several times[5]. Brother of Josef, Leon, and his wife Hanka were killed in Częstochowa on March 21st, 1943[6]. Szlama Josef was in Buchenwal till the end of WWII. Szlama Josef, Emil and Tusia survived the Holocaust. Emil moved to New Zealand, Tusia came to Australia.

Szlama Josef did not get Diploma after graduation because of WWII. In 1946 he sent several letters to the University of Bologna saying that “without the diploma I can not exercise my profession”[7]. According to the letters, he asked the secretariat of the University to send the Diploma to his house in Laudsberg (Germany). However, he did not get any replies. “I have already written twice to the secretariat requesting to send me my diploma <…> So far I have not received any reply”[8], – he wrote in the letter. Then Szlama Josef Fromer asked the University to deliver his Diploma to his brother Emil to New Zealand, because he was moving to Australia at that time. He settled in Sydney. Szlama Josef Fromer died in Sydney on April 3rd, 2012 at the age of 97[9].

Laudsberg on 9 November 1945
To the Secretariat of the University of Bologna. 10926
I kindly ask you to send me my diploma of doctor of medicine
And Surgery. I took the degree examination on 3 July 1939 at the above-mentioned university,
with one hundred points. Since I was told at the secretariat at the time that the diploma
could not be obtained before September 1939, I left for home ,
with the intention of writing after two months to the university to send me
the diploma. Because of the war, however, I was unable to do so. After all
I was imprisoned in the concentration camp in Buchenwal, Germany
as a political prisoner from the beginning of the war until the end of it.
Now as private persons there is no mail between Germany and Italy I
Please kindly send the diploma to the address Mrs Gafan,
which I add below, who is an American citizen and employed by the
American A.J.D.C. As such she is entitled to the
Military Post. Graduation rates were paid even before
of my departure home in 1939. I add the identity card written
in English and issued by the American "Joint" in Laudsberg. In the hope
that you will send me my diploma as soon as possible.
With regards
Dr. Szlama Josef Fromer
