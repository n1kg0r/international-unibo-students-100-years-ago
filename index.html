Being international students
100 years ago
SCHOLARLY EDITING AND DIGITAL TEXTUALITY PROJECT
University of Bologna

How did international students of the past live the admission process to University of Bologna? This is the question raised by our group to create this project about five Jewish students who studied at the University of Bologna between 1920 and 1940. We have worked on demonstrating not only the bureaucratic processes in the past, but also to showcase unique life paths hidden in numerous archival documents. Observed students are from different countries and backgrounds, but all of them are enrolled in the Faculties of Medicine and Surgery, or Sciences. The objects of our studies were the digitized documents in the official folders of the international students. A crucial aspect of our project is the historical context, which is why we have written biographies of each student. The target audience of this project are scholars and researchers who are interested in studying the experience of international students in Italy.

The project “Being International students 100 years ago” gives an understanding not only of international students studying at UNIBO in general, but of the history of Italy juste before the start of WWII. This project was launched by five students of the Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge program of the University of Bologna. Three of them are foreign students coming from Russia, Kazakhstan and China, and the other two are Italians. The project was made with the support of the Archivio storico dell'Università di Bologna for our course in Scholarly Editing and Digital Textuality.
