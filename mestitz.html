Mihaly Mestitz (1909 — 1994)
Studied Medicine and Surgery at the University of Bologna
Romania
BIOGRAPHY

Mihaly Mestitz was born in June 2, 1909 to Henrik and Laszio Elena in Targu Mures in Marosvásárhely (Târgu Mureș) in the historical region of Transylvania (Austria-Hungary, then Romania). The Mestitz’s, of Jewish origin, were a well-known Marosvásárhely family. They owned a big furniture factory in the city, which was founded by Mihaly's grandfather of the same name. The elder Mestitz's son, named Henrik Mihaly, had five children, of whom only three reached adulthood: Mihaly, Julia (Scheiner after her husband) and Andras.
Read more

Mihaly or Misi (how he was called by the family) attended Protestant high school in Marosvásárhely (Târgu Mureș now). In 1927 he was sent to Budapest to graduate school because he failed graduation test at school in Marosvásárhely[1]. He was good at German language and literature[2]. Then Mihaly spent a year in Munich and another year in Arad in a furniture factory. When he was 21 years old he came back to Marosvásárhely to work in the family furniture store.

According to memories of his sister Mihaly always wanted to become a doctor. “In the end Misi said he wasn't willing to do this his entire life: waiting for customers, packing the commodities”,[3] – his sister Julia told in the interview. Eventually, his father agreed with decision of Mihaly to study medicine. “He graduated in Hungary, and that wasn't enough to enter a Romanian university. He met a guy here who had been to Italy, and he told him a few things. Misi then went to Italy”.[4] Mihaly Mestitz went to the University of Bologna and completed the prescribed six-year period of study for the degree in Medicine and Surgery[5]. Then Mihaly started second degree in 1937 and was enrolled in the 3rd year of the faculty of pharmacy undergraduate course of the University of Bologna[6]. He didn’t finish it.

During studies at the University of Bologna Mihaly met Clara Maletti. She was a Catholic. “Her father, a wealthy man, was a pharmacist in a small town near Bologna. Misi rented a car every week and went there. They got married in 1938 in Bologna[7]. Misi and Clara moved back from Italy to Marosvásárhely next year. He got hired at the sanatorium as a surgeon.[8] Also a girl was born.

During the Second World War, northern Transylvania was occupied by the Hungarian fascists, who deported 150,000 Jews to Auschwitz in 1944. Julia, sister of Mihaly Mestitz, was deported to Auschwitz-Birkenau together with her mother. Mihaly convinced the gendarme not to take him along with the rest of the Jews. “The gendarme couldn't [or wouldn't] read all the documents my brother presented him to prove to him they didn't have to go. My brother didn't want to go, he persevered, as he wasn't a weak person, and he had a wife and a child. The gendarme gave in eventually”.[9] Mihaly successfully hid during the deportations in 1944 and hid until the entry of Soviet troops. However, his sister and his mother could not prove the same. His mother died in concentration camp. “Suddenly I noticed that mom, my girlfriend's mother and a seven-year old boy who lived in the same tent back in the brickyard, were going away. We didn't even have time to say goodbye. I asked a soldier where they were taking them. He said they were being taken to the showers by car. Their entire system was built on lies because if they had told us 'we are taking them away and you will never see them again', they would have had hysteria to face”. [10]Julia spent seven and a half months in Auschwitz. Then she was taken to Birkenau, and then to Ravensbruck. She was liberated from a Concentration camp in Magdeburg. Andras, brother of Mihaly, was called up for labor service to Máramarossziget in 1942. From there he was deported to concentration camp in Mauthausen and then to another one Günskirchen in 1944. He and Julia survived and returned home.[11]

A son of Mihaly and Clara was born in 1946. Next year they moved to Italy and bought a house in Bologna. In 1949 the Mestitz furniture factory was nationalized by the communist authorities. Mihaly worked as a doctor and received patients in an office. He stopped doing surgery because internal medicine went well. Mihaly Mestitz had a heart attack in 1994 and died.[12] His brother Andras moved to the USA in 1961 in Minneapolis and worked there as an accountant. He died in 2008. Only Julia Scheiner lived her life at home in Marosvásárhely.
